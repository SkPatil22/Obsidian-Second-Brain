# Running Shoes — Flat Feet (Under $200)

Research for Sachet: jogging + boxing use, super flat feet, stable (no foot-shift), under $200.

## The Core Tension

Running shoes and boxing shoes pull in opposite directions. Running shoes stack heel cushion (great for pavement shock, bad for boxing pivots — you lose ground feel and lateral stability). Boxing shoes are flat, grippy, and ankle-hugging. **Cross-training shoes are the right compromise** for someone doing both without buying two pairs.

Key flat-foot requirements: medial post or dual-density foam to resist overpronation, low-to-moderate heel drop (3–8mm), wide forefoot, firm heel counter.

## Top Picks

### 1. Nike Free Metcon 6 — ~$130 ⭐ Best All-Rounder
The go-to for gym-to-jog use. Flexible split forefoot for boxing footwork and HIIT; firm heel counter with higher sidewalls locks the foot laterally; soft foam midsole absorbs jog impact. The forefoot flex grooves mean your foot can spread on pivots without shifting inside the shoe. Not ideal for long runs (5+ miles) but perfect for the boxing + jogging combo.
- Drop: ~4mm | Weight: ~9.3 oz | Width: standard/wide available
- **Flat-feet note:** moderate medial support; not a dedicated stability shoe, but the wide platform compensates.

### 2. Reebok Nano X5 Edge — ~$150 Best Overall Stability
Best-in-class lateral stability. ~7mm drop, wide heel platform, firm midsole that doesn't compress under lateral load. Excellent for boxing footwork because the base doesn't rock. Slightly less flexible in the forefoot than the Metcon, so boxing drills feel a bit stiffer — but the stability payoff is real for flat feet.
- Drop: ~7mm | Weight: ~10.5 oz
- **Flat-feet note:** widest platform of the group; high sidewalls prevent foot rollout.

### 3. NOBULL Outwork Edge — ~$149 Best for Flat/Wide Feet
Rigid heel counter + 4mm drop = very grounded feel. Anatomical last means the shoe doesn't taper aggressively at the toe, which is critical for flat feet (flatter feet tend to spread). Good running feel up to ~3–4 miles. Strong boxing pivot stability.
- Drop: 4mm | Weight: ~10 oz
- **Flat-feet note:** anatomical toe box is the standout feature here.

## Buying Decision

**Buy one pair and do both → Nike Free Metcon 6 or Reebok Nano X5 Edge.**

The Metcon 6 wins on boxing feel (forefoot flex, lower price). The Nano X5 Edge wins on stability for flat feet (wider base, firmer lateral walls). If foot shift is your primary concern during boxing, go Nano X5 Edge. If you jog more than you box, go Metcon 6.

**Buy two pairs (one per activity) → Nano X5 Edge for boxing/gym + Brooks Adrenaline GTS 25 for runs.** The GTS 25 is the best pure running shoe for flat feet in this budget by a wide margin.
