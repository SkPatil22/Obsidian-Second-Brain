# Seattle Trip — Jul 21–25, 2026

5 days / 4 nights, **Tue–Sat**. Goal: downtown Seattle + [[Olympic National Park]] + [[Mount Rainier National Park]]. Destinations: [[Pike Place Market]], [[Olympic National Park]], [[Mount Rainier National Park]].

> [!info] Two lucky breaks on these dates
> 1. **Rainier needs no timed-entry reservation in 2026** (the pilot was cancelled) — just show up, but go early for parking.
> 2. Your dates hit the **start of Paradise wildflower peak** (last week of July). And Tue–Sat means the parks fall on **weekdays** — the best days for parking.

## Recommended itinerary

| Day | Plan | Why |
|-----|------|-----|
| **Tue 21** | Arrive · **Downtown Seattle** — [[Pike Place Market]], waterfront, dinner | Ease in; city day |
| **Wed 22** | **Mount Rainier** day trip — leave Seattle ~6am, Paradise by ~8:30, Skyline Trail + wildflowers | Weekday = parking; ~2.5h each way |
| **Thu 23** | **Olympic** — Hurricane Ridge → Lake Crescent (Marymere Falls) → Sol Duc Falls · overnight **Port Angeles** | North park loop; 1 night out |
| **Fri 24** | **Olympic** — Hoh Rainforest (Hall of Mosses) → Ruby Beach → drive back to Seattle | Rainforest + coast; weekday |
| **Sat 25** | Seattle morning (coffee, a museum or more market) · depart | Sat = stay out of crowded parks |

_Alt: swap Wed for an overnight near Ashford to catch **sunset wildflowers** at Paradise (lots empty out 3–5pm). More magic, one more hotel change._

## Downtown Seattle — recs
Anchor is [[Pike Place Market]]. Eat: **Pike Place Chowder**, **Sushi Kashiba** (best sushi — book ahead), **The Pink Door** (Italian + live music/burlesque, book), **Beecher's** (mac & cheese), **Le Panier** (French pastries), **Oriental Mart** (Filipino), **El Borracho** (tacos). Beyond the market: waterfront + Seattle Aquarium/Great Wheel, Pioneer Square, a quick trip up the Space Needle or skip for Kerry Park views.

## Olympic National Park — recs
See [[Olympic National Park]]. Two-day flow with a Port Angeles night: **Hurricane Ridge** (alpine views), **Lake Crescent** (Marymere Falls easy; Mount Storm King if ambitious), **Sol Duc Falls**, **Hoh Rainforest** (Hall of Mosses), **Ruby Beach** (sea stacks). Three ecosystems — mountains, rainforest, wild coast — in one park.

## Mount Rainier — recs
See [[Mount Rainier National Park]]. Base day at **Paradise**: the **Skyline Trail** loop through wildflower meadows with the glacier above. Arrive by ~8:30am for parking (or come for the 3–5pm → sunset window). **Sunrise** area (NE, opens early July) if you have extra time.

## Logistics
- **Rental car is essential** — no transit to the parks.
- **Park pass:** one **America the Beautiful** pass ($80) covers both parks and pays off vs. $30/park.
- **Weather (late July):** Seattle warm/dry (~75°F); mountains cooler; Olympic coast can be foggy/wet — pack layers + a rain shell + sun protection for Rainier snowfields.
- **Cell service is poor** in both parks — download offline maps.
