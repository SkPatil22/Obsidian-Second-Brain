# Raw research — Raspberry Chocolate Cake (2026-06-25)

Distilled research notes gathered for the recipe + technique pages. Not a verbatim source; key findings + provenance.

## Moisture (from-scratch chocolate cake)
- Hot liquid (boiling water or hot coffee) in the batter dissolves sugar, blooms the cocoa, and activates leavening so the cake sets fast and stays moist. Coffee deepens chocolate flavor without tasting of coffee.
- Oil (vs all butter) gives a more reliably moist, tender crumb that stays soft cold.
- Buttermilk adds tang + tenderness (reacts with baking soda).

## Preventing raspberries from sinking
- It's berry weight vs batter body. Pourable batter lets them fall; scoopable batter suspends them.
- Dry berries thoroughly (excess water = heavier, slippery).
- Toss berries in a spoon of flour/starch for grip.
- Par-bake trick: spread a thin layer of plain batter, bake a few minutes until just set, then fold berries into the rest and pour over.

## Fresh vs frozen raspberries
- Frozen: hold shape in cakes/muffins, less bleeding — add frozen, do NOT thaw.
- Fresh: best for fillings, compote, and final décor (appearance/structure).

## Ganache / drip
- Use high-fat heavy/whipping cream for a rich, stable (non-grainy) ganache.
- Chill the assembled cake ~1 hr; use cooled (not hot) ganache. Warm cake + warm ganache = puddle; cold cake + cooled ganache = controlled drip.
