# Raw research — Seattle / Olympic / Rainier trip (gathered 2026-06-25)

For trip dates **Jul 21–25, 2026 (Tue–Sat)**. Distilled findings + provenance.

## Mount Rainier
- **No timed-entry reservation required anywhere in the park in 2026** (NPS confirmed; the 2024–25 pilot was cancelled). No advance entry slot needed for Paradise or Sunrise.
- Constraint is **parking**: Paradise lots fill ~8–9am summer weekends (clear Saturdays by 8am); Sunrise fills ~10am. **Tue–Thu mornings** are the safest. Lots free up ~3–5pm.
- **Wildflowers at Paradise peak last week of July → first week of August** — trip dates are right at the start. Sunset at Paradise during bloom is a top pick.
- **Sunrise Road** opens late June/early July (snowmelt dependent).
- ~2–2.5 hr drive from Seattle to the Nisqually (SW) entrance / Paradise.

## Olympic National Park
- 2 days is the sweet spot. Hoh Rainforest ~4 hr from Seattle; whole park 3–5 hr depending on area.
- **Bases**: Port Angeles (best all-rounder — Hurricane Ridge, Lake Crescent, restaurants); Forks (coast + Hoh).
- Highlights: **Hurricane Ridge** (alpine views/meadows), **Lake Crescent** (Marymere Falls easy; Mount Storm King steep), **Sol Duc Falls**, **Hoh Rainforest** (Hall of Mosses), **Rialto Beach / Ruby Beach** (sea stacks).
- Best season July–Sept (roads open, driest).

## Downtown Seattle / Pike Place Market
- Eat: **Pike Place Chowder** (lines), **Sushi Kashiba** (top sushi, book), **The Pink Door** (Italian + live music/burlesque), **Beecher's Handmade Cheese** (mac & cheese), **Le Panier** (French pastries), **Oriental Mart** (Filipino), **El Borracho** (tacos, veg-friendly), **Market Grill** (salmon sandwich).
- Market itself: 75+ food stalls, buskers, flowers/produce, water + Olympic Mtn views; classic spot for visitors.
